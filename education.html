Skills

  • ⚬ Developed ability to think critically and solve complex problems

  • ⚬ Enhanced logical thinking

Content

Weekly Lab Exercises
The lab quizzes which covered all the key concepts in our lectures. They were essential in advancing my problem-solving capabilities and perseverance, as some questions really stretched my imagination and creativity.

Figure 3.1 demonstrates a lab question on box proofs, covering the logical operation: contra-constructive. Box proofs are a great way of proving that a propositional formula holds. Contra-constructive allows us to work backwards through the assumption of a propositional formula being false. From this, we derive a contradiction.
Mid & End-term Tests
Both tests were extremely pleasant and enjoyable to complete. The mid and end term tests had two parts to them and I managed to achieve 100% overall for my mid-term test.

Figure 3.2 demonstrates a very complex proof not from my exams, but an extra set of exercises provided by the module. It was challenging but extremely fun trying to achieve the shortest and most efficient box proof possible. 18 lines was the shortest possible and was a satisfactory proof for the propositional formula.

I did not know how to solve propositional formula using box proofs. There are so many clever rules to ultilise in order to achieve the shortest proofs possible. Rules which I did not know about are for example the implication rule and then contra-constructive rule.
